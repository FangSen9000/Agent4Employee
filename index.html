Abstract

This study presents a systematic investigation into gender bias manifestation within autonomous agent-based employment systems, specifically focusing on the Agentic Employment Infrastructure platform powered by Large Language Models (LLMs). Through analysis of 400 virtual employee profiles over a 10-year simulation period, we examine the potential perpetuation of gender biases in automated decision-making processes across four critical domains: task allocation, performance evaluation, promotion trajectories, and compensation structures. Our findings reveal significant gender-based disparities, with the experimental condition demonstrating a concerning bias score of 59.79/100 (there's a 9.79% shift from neutral). The study contributes to the growing discourse on algorithmic fairness by identifying specific mechanisms through which gender bias manifests in AI-driven employment systems.
